What is Explorate?

When I was 16, I thought I found the perfect volunteering experience – saving sea turtles in Costa Rica! The program seemed to have everything I wanted, including unique jungle housing, time spent closely examining and saving sea turtles, exciting surf excursions, and a guaranteed team coordinator who would help me navigate a foreign land and new experiences. But my experience was nothing like what I had imagined. On top of an uninterested coordinator and crowded, bug-filled housing, I only got to spend 10 minutes (in 20 days!) monitoring the sea turtles. I returned home, disappointed, but not disheartened. Hoping to redeem Costa Rica, I left for a similar excursion to Ecuador. This trip promised time cultivating indigenous plant species and encouraging natural bird behavior to maintain the biodiversity of the Galapagos Islands.

Despite having done extensive research this time, I was, again, disappointed. No cell service, no coordinator within reach and, instead of maintaining the fragile ecosystem, I spent most days peeling coffee beans in the kitchen. Returning home, I was frustrated by the misrepresentation of both trips. My passion for volunteering remained, but how could I go on another trip like these two? If only I had known more beforehand, I could have chosen better, and been better prepared when I arrived. If only I was in contact with people who had gone before me instead of feeling like I’m alone on my own path, these trips could have been the excursions of a lifetime! I couldn’t let anyone else make the same mistakes I did. So, I turned the “if only’s” into one simple word: Explorate."


How I got here?

It was inspired by my personal international volunteering experience and how I found that my expectation and reality didn’t really line up. I wanted to create a platform where volunteers could be more prepared and inspired for their trip by reading other’s stories. To proceed, I conducted a semi-structured interview with 15 other volunteers. Then, I interviewed NGO in developing countries about their needs to gain an understanding of the covid context to aid future planning. From here, I synthesized the interviews to craft my mission and value statements.

Prototype testing

Review

I provided 3 options for users to choose from to best design the display of the review, which is one of the main sections of my website. Many favored the last option because they liked having the options to see comments about specific parts of the experience (ex. accomodation) in a more organized format with the drop-down button.

Connect

I showed users in this demo how they would be able to connect with one another and asked whether they would be interested in using this feature. All of them gave positive feedback! Therefore, aside from making it a one-way interaction of reviews, I aim to build two-way connections within the international volunteering community and make the experience less dauting.

Filter & search

I tested whether users would prefer less or more options when they search. Most would like fewer options to start the search with and have more options in the filter available when browsing.

Competitive analysis

After comparing with the two leading organizations in international volunteering, I claimed our niche in:

✅ Focus on forming communities

✅ Pledge to focus on meaningful volunteering opportunities and design alternatives to voluntourism (mainly focus on alternative livelihood programs)

✅ We care about communities beyond international volunteers, specifically the communities that volunteers are devoting their time to
