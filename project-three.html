Storybytes

Class Project

How might we design a tool for journalists to create and use GIFs to tell stories and make storytelling more efficient and attractive?

  • Partner

    Knight lab studio

  • Scope

    10 weeks

  • Categories

    Mobile app, digital journalism, design, technology, media, storytelling

  • Role

    Design team member (group of 5)

  • Tasks

    User interview, persona, journey map, SMART goals and team retro, mockups, onboarding design, app store summary

  • Tools

    Figma, zoom, miro, canva, typeform, survey monkey, Xmind

  • Skills

    UX and UI design, product management, teamwork, content writing, interview synthesis, presentation

Problem

  • As the open-web continues to die, storytellers need tools that can create quick sharable artifacts to visualize their stories
    for a variety of social networks and platforms.

    Visual news stories often lack efficient storytelling and are platform-locked. It can often be time consuming and potentially frustrating for journalists when they have to create different posts for different platforms.

Process

I came into the project midway through so I was mostly involved in making the idea come into reality.

“The TikTok platform determines the popularity of a video by the
percentage of people that finish watching the video. So the shorter
a video is, the more successful it could be” --- news journalist in China

“ Use hashtags on Instagram and track what’s most reposted to
figure out what people are interested in. For instagram, because
the algorithm is changing, people are constantly finding ways to
keep their post relevant” ---Spoon journalist

I first interviewed journalists to understand their workflow, such as the technology they use and how they assess success,
and how their needs may align with our GIF tool.The main insights show that our deliverable needs to be a concise visual display that can be spread quickly on social platforms with mobile devices.

From there, I made some final GIF product mockups focusing on the 5W framework for quick and easy drafting on the jouranlist’s side. I put myself in the role of a journalist reporting news stories. First, I synthesized news articles into the 5W framework. Then, I selected visuals that represent the outline. Finally, I used Adobe Premiere to create the design of the examples.

Onboarding design of StoryBytes

When creating the onboarding design, I focused on making the process concise and clear.
It walks the users through the process without having them feel locked in.
